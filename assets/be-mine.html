Spec Sheet: "Be Mine" Matcha Latte (8oz)

Yield: 1 x 8oz Beverage
Temp Target: 170°F - 180°F [web:3]
Whisk Time: ~30 Seconds
Ingredients:
- 3.0g Sifted Matcha
- 30.0g Water
- 1 Pump Yuzu Syrup (Optional)
- ~6oz Milk (Whole, 2%, or Lactose-Free)

Standard Operating Procedure

  1. Prep the Whisk: Fill a cup with hot water and submerge the tines of the bamboo whisk to soften them, which prevents the delicate bamboo from snapping during use [web:16][web:18].
  2. Sift & Dose: Place your bowl on a high-precision coffee scale [web:16]. Sift exactly 3.0g of matcha through a fine mesh sieve into the bowl; skipping this guarantees a gritty, clumpy drink [web:17][web:18].
  3. Water Addition: Tare the scale and pour exactly 30.0g of 170°F-180°F water over the powder.
  4. Whisking Technique: Hold the whisk comfortably and use your wrist—not your arm—to aggressively whisk in a "W" or "M" motion [web:18]. Hover the whisk slightly above the bottom to avoid grinding the tines [web:18]. Whisk for about 30 seconds.
  5. Surface Correction: Slowly lift the whisk toward the surface while continuing the motion to break up any large surface bubbles, leaving behind a smooth microfoam [web:16].
  6. Build the Base: Pour the matcha concentrate and 1 pump of yuzu syrup into the serving cup. Visual cue of a properly done matcha: a smooth, fully suspended liquid with dense microfoam and zero dry powder left in your mixing bowl [web:18].
  7. Milk Steaming: Purge your steam wand. Steam your milk in a 12oz pitcher to a wet-paint microfoam texture, identical to flat white/latte art standards.
  8. Pour: Integrate the milk into the matcha base slowly, then drop close to the surface to pour your heart design.

Professional Pointers & Scaling

  • Scaling for Two (16oz total): If you need to make two drinks at once, do not simply double the dose in a small bowl. You must use a large, commercial-width chawan to accommodate 6.0g of matcha and 60.0g of water so the whisk has room to aerate the liquid properly [web:16][web:18]. If your bowl is too small, you must whisk them sequentially.
  • Why Sifting Matters: Matcha does not dissolve; it suspends in water [web:18]. Un-sifted matcha will instantly lock into dry clumps when water hits it, ruining the texture [web:17][web:18].
