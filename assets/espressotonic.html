Spec Sheet: Espresso Tonic (10oz - 12oz Glass)

Yield: 1 x 10-12oz Beverage
Glassware: Highball or Collins Glass [web:62]
Ratio: 1 part Espresso : 3 parts Tonic [web:60]
Ingredients:
- 2 Shots Espresso (~40g / 1.5oz)
- 120g - 150g Premium Tonic Water (4-5oz) [web:60][web:62]
- 1 Pump Yuzu or Simple Syrup (Optional)
- ~120g Ice (Enough to fill the glass)

Standard Operating Procedure

  1. Prep the Base: Fill your 10-12oz glass to the rim with ice. Using plenty of ice keeps the tonic violently cold, which suppresses the foaming reaction [web:65][web:68].
  2. Add Tonic: Slowly pour exactly 120g to 150g (4-5oz) of chilled tonic water over the ice. Do not stir it. Let it settle.
  3. Pull & Mix Espresso: Pull a double shot of espresso (40g) into a small pouring pitcher or demitasse. If the customer requested syrup (yuzu is highly recommended), pump it directly into the hot espresso and give it a quick swirl to dissolve.
  4. The Float (Crucial Step): Slowly and gently pour the espresso-syrup mixture over the back of a bar spoon, or aim directly for an exposed ice cube floating at the surface [web:60][web:65][web:68].
  5. Serve Layered: Do not stir. Serve the drink layered. The customer will experience the visual appeal of the descending crema and can gently swirl the glass themselves before drinking.

Professional Pointers & Science

  • Foam Prevention: The CO2 in carbonated water reacts violently with the CO2 in espresso crema, amplified by the heat of the shot [web:65][web:68]. If you pour too fast or dump the espresso straight into the tonic pool, it will instantly bubble over the sides of the glass [web:65][web:68]. Pouring over a spoon breaks the fall and minimizes agitation [web:60][web:68].
  • Syrup Integration: Syrup is too dense to mix into cold tonic without aggressive stirring (which kills the bubbles). Always dissolve the syrup in the hot espresso first.
  • Bean Choice: If you have options, use a washed, fruit-forward, or floral espresso bean (like an Ethiopian or Kenyan) [web:60]. Dark, earthy roasts clash terribly with the quinine bitterness in tonic water.
