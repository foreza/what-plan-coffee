Spec Sheet: "Chai Ni Made" Dirty Chai Latte (8oz)

Yield: 1 x 8oz Beverage
Tea Concentrate: 100ml pre-steeped
Espresso: 2 Shots (Double)
Per Drink Ingredients:
- 100ml Chai Concentrate
- 2 Shots Espresso (~38-40g)
- 1 Pump Simple Syrup
- ~4oz Milk (Whole, 2%, or Lactose-Free)

Commercial Half-Batch Prep: Yields 3 Servings

Yield: 300ml concentrate. Note: Leaves will absorb ~120g of water [web:31].

  1. Measure 60g of loose chai tea leaves into a heat-safe vessel.
  2. Bring 420ml of water to a rolling boil (212°F).
  3. Pour boiling water over the chai leaves and stir gently to ensure all leaves are saturated.
  4. Let steep for exactly 5 minutes. Set a timer.
  5. Strain aggressively through a fine mesh strainer or China cap, pressing the wet leaves to extract all liquid. You will yield roughly 300ml of concentrate.
  6. Cool to room temperature before covering. Store in an airtight glass container in the refrigerator [web:41][web:45].

Standard Operating Procedure (Per Drink)

  1. Measure & Pour: Pour exactly 100ml of your chilled chai concentrate into a small pitcher or cup.
  2. Pull Double Espresso: Pull 2 shots of espresso directly into the chai concentrate (yielding ~38–40g of espresso). The heat from the espresso will begin to temper the chilled chai.
  3. Add Sweetener: Add 1 pump of simple syrup and stir gently to combine.
  4. Preheat Steam Wand: Purge your steam wand to clear condensation.
  5. Steam Milk: Fill a 12oz pitcher with ~4oz of milk (to the base of the spout). Steam to a wet-paint microfoam texture.
  6. Pour & Serve: Slowly pour the steamed milk into the espresso–chai base to incorporate, then drop close to the surface to execute a heart design.

Professional Notes

  • Shelf Life: Homemade, unsweetened chai concentrate without milk stored in an airtight glass container in the fridge will last 4 to 5 days [web:45]. If it hits day 6, dump it.
  • Double Shots Only: A single shot will still be buried by 100ml of proper chai concentrate. Maintain the double shot standard to ensure the espresso cuts through the spices.
